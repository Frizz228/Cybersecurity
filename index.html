Why is Cybersecurity Important?

With the development of digital technologies, the number of cyber threats is also increasing. Hackers are constantly looking for vulnerabilities in protection systems to gain access to personal data, financial information, or simply cause harm. Knowledge of cybersecurity basics helps protect yourself from such attacks.

Current Global Threat Level

Low Medium High Critical

The current threat level is HIGH due to increased ransomware attacks and phishing campaigns targeting remote workers.

Main Threats on the Internet

Phishing

Fraudulent emails and websites that mimic legitimate resources to obtain your login credentials.

Malware

Viruses, trojans, spyware, and ransomware that can damage your system or steal data.

Password Attacks

Using weak passwords or reusing them makes your accounts vulnerable to brute force attacks.

Social Engineering

Manipulating people to obtain confidential information or access to systems.

How to Protect Yourself from Hacking

Strong Passwords

Use complex passwords at least 12 characters long with letters, numbers, and special characters.

Two-Factor Authentication

Enable 2FA wherever possible for an additional layer of protection.

Regular Updates

Always update your operating system and applications to the latest versions.

Caution with Links

Do not click on suspicious links or download files from untrusted sources.

Step-by-Step Guide to Protection Against Hacking

1

Assess Your Current Security

Check which accounts have weak or reused passwords. Use a password manager to create and store strong passwords.

2

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

For important accounts (email, banking applications, social networks), be sure to enable two-factor authentication.

3

Update Software

Make sure your operating system, browser, and all applications are updated to the latest versions. Many updates contain security vulnerability fixes.

4

Be Careful with Email

Do not open attachments or click on links in suspicious emails. Phishing remains one of the most common hacking methods.

5

Use VPN on Public Networks

When connecting to public Wi-Fi networks, use a VPN to encrypt your internet traffic and protect against data interception.

6

Regularly Backup Your Data

Create backups of important data on external media or in cloud storage. This will protect you from data loss in case of a ransomware attack.

Security Tip

Regularly check privacy settings on social networks and limit the amount of personal information available publicly. The less information about you is available in open access, the more difficult it is for attackers to conduct a targeted attack.
